Essential knowledge for anyone working in or managing a Security Operations Center. This note covers the fundamental concepts, organizational structures, processes, and best practices that make SOCs effective.

## Key Topics Covered

- **SOC Mission & Structure**: Understanding the purpose and organization of modern SOCs
- **Core Processes**: Collection → Detection → Triage → Investigation → Improvement
- **Industry Reports**: Verizon DBIR and Mandiant M-Trends as essential reading
- **Documentation Framework**: Policies, standards, procedures, guidelines, and playbooks
- **Metrics & Communication**: Measuring SOC effectiveness and communicating value
- **Tiered vs Tierless Models**: Different approaches to SOC organization

## Why This Matters

SOCs are the front line of organizational cybersecurity defense. Understanding how they work, what makes them effective, and how to measure their success is crucial for anyone in cybersecurity. These fundamentals provide the foundation for building, operating, or improving SOC capabilities.

## Key References

- **Verizon Data Breach Investigations Report (DBIR) 2025**: Annual comprehensive analysis of security incidents and data breaches
- **Mandiant M-Trends Report 2025**: Advanced threat intelligence and incident response insights from Google Cloud Security
- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework**: Risk management framework for critical infrastructure cybersecurity
- **MITRE ATT&CK Framework**: Knowledge base of adversary tactics and techniques based on real-world observations

## 2025 Key Statistics & Trends

### Verizon DBIR 2025 Highlights
- **Third-party involvement** in breaches has **doubled** compared to last year, driven by vulnerability exploitation and business interruptions
- **Significant increase** in attackers exploiting vulnerabilities to gain initial access and cause security breaches
- **Less than half** of perimeter-device vulnerabilities were fully remediated by organizations, with almost half remaining unresolved
- **Notable rise** in ransomware presence across all breaches analyzed

### Mandiant M-Trends 2025 Highlights
- **37% of investigations** showed evidence of data theft (consistent with 2023)
- **11% of cases** involved data theft extortion without ransomware deployment
- **6% of cases** involved multi-faceted extortion (both data theft and ransomware encryption)
- **APT28 (Russian)** conducted selective data theft focusing on personnel data and geopolitical content
- **APT41 (Chinese)** used SQLULDR2 and PINEGROVE tools for systematic large-scale data exfiltration via OneDrive
