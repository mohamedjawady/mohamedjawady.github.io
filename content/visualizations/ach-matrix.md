Analysis of Competing Hypotheses, developed by Richards Heuer at the CIA, is the structured analytic technique that most directly attacks confirmation bias. Instead of building a case for the explanation you already favor, you list every plausible hypothesis, score each piece of evidence against all of them, and then reject hypotheses rather than confirm them. The judgment goes to the hypothesis with the least inconsistent evidence.

The matrix below is preloaded with a scenario: your organization's public web server was defaced with hacktivist messaging days after a geopolitical flashpoint. Four explanations compete. Click any cell to re-score it and watch the inconsistency counts change. Click an evidence row to read the analyst note behind its suggested scoring.
