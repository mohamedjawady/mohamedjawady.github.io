A threat model built the target-centric way starts at the center, with what the organization has, and works outward to the adversaries those assets attract. This explorer models Northwind Analytics, a fictional climate-data SaaS company, with three crown jewels: its proprietary forecasting models, its customer datasets, and the availability of the platform itself.

Pivot on the assets to see which adversary each crown jewel attracts and why, then switch to pivoting on the adversaries to see their likely tooling, their operational dependencies, and the trigger events that would raise your risk of being targeted. The lesson of the two views together is that different crown jewels attract different adversaries for different reasons, so the actors worth watching select themselves once you have named what you actually have to lose.
